Myths


1. Dengue mosquitoes breed in drains, ponds, puddles
a. Aedes mosquito responsible for transmission of dengue breeds only in clean water.


2. All cases of dengue should be hospitalized
a. Majority of the cases of dengue can be treated at home. Hospitalization is required only if a case of dengue has complications like vomiting, bleeding, shock or organ failure


3. Decrease in platelets is due to dengue only
a. Platelets decrease in most of the viral fever, typhoid, malaria and many other causes. Dengue is just one of the reasons of low platelets


4. All cases of dengue need blood and platelets
a. Blood or platelets are required in a case of dengue if the patient has bleeding or platelets have decreased to less than 10000/cmm


5. All cases of dengue will die
a. Case fatality rate of dengue is less than 1% which means 1 out of 100 cases can die. The death is mostly seen in cases which already have some other disease too


6. Fogging is the only way to control dengue
a. Fogging is the spray of insecticides in form of fog. Fogging can kill mosquitoes which are outside.
b. Fogging is only useful when the Aedes have increased a lot.
c. Normally Aedes are found inside the houses.
d. FIdeally, the doors and windows of the houses should be opened to allow the fog to enter the houses and vehicle should be driving at a very slow speed for fog to disperse properly
e. Household repellents can be effective in prevention of bite by Aedes


7. Prevention of dengue is responsibility of Health Department
a. Prevention of dengue is responsibility of each household and office since Aedes breeds inside or surrounding the houses in water containers, refrigerator trays, tyres, tanks etc
b. Efforts of Department of Health and Local Govt can bear the fruit if all residents prevent the breeding opportunities of aedes by cleaning all breeding places once a week
