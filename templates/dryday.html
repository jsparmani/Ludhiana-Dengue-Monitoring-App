Weekly Dry Day


● The adult aedes mosquito develops in one week (average 7-10 days) from egg
● The eggs of aedes can stick to the walls of the water container and survive for more than one year
● If the containers are dried once a week then the development of adult mosquito from the egg can be prevented
● The Department of Health and Family Welfare has declared EVERY FRIDAY A DRY DAY Which means that each household and office should ensure that the cooler, water containers, flower vases, tyres, flower pots, water tanks and all those places where clean water is stored should be cleaned once a week. Thus breeding of aedes can be prevented.
    a. In the water containers, which can't be emptied, water can be sprayed with larvicides like Temephos or Bti or oil can be sprinkled on the water.
    b. Oil when sprinkled on water forms a thin layer and cuts off oxygen supply to the larvae and pupae thus killing them and preventing the development of aedes.
